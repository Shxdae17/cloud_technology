Born September 25, 1983, Donald McKinley Glover Jr., also known by his stage name Childish Gambino, is an American actor, comedian, singer, rapper, writer, director, and producer. Growing up Glover, alongside his younger brother Stephen who would later become a writer and collaborate with him and his siter Brianne, was born and raised on a Air Force Base where his father was stationed. Glover was raised as a Jehovah's witness, however he is no longer religious. Through 2006-2010, after working in Derrick Comedy while studying at New York University, Glover was hired at age 23 by Tina Fey as a writer for the NBC sitcom 30 Rock. In 2009, Glover played in works such as a former high school student in the sitcom 'Community','Mystery Team', and many more. Despite his rise in acting and comedy, many speculated after starring in the first five episodes of 'Community',Goover was leaving to persue his now retired music career. However, this speculation was wrong as he stated his character would not serve the show, the audience, or himself as an actor. Glover earned his stage name, Childish Gambino, which he used to start his music career from the famous Wu-Tang Clan name generator. With this new stage name, Gambino would later release mixtapes, EP and finally his debut studio album 'Camp' charting Billboard charts. Gambino who is now retired from music, has decided to return to the television screen. Producing and starring in works such as 'Atlanta','Swarm', 'Spider-Man:Homecoming'. Glover is a mutiple winner of 'Golden Globes', 'Grammy Award', and 'Emmy Award'.
